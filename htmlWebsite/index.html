Total Deaths by Cause

World, 1990-2019

Why this visualization?
The visualization was chosen to provide a clear and easy-to-understand overview of the burden of disease faced by humans globally. It shows the number of deaths from various causes over a 30-year period in a visually appealing way that makes it easier for viewers to interpret the data.

What is it visualizing?
The visualization displays the total number of deaths recorded in the world for specific causes between 1990 and 2019. It provides a visual representation of the number of deaths as the area of circles, with larger circles indicating higher numbers of deaths.

How is it visualizing?
The visualization uses a circle-packing technique, which arranges circles within a container so that they do not overlap. The circles are sized according to the number of deaths recorded for each cause, with the largest circles indicating the highest numbers of deaths. This method allows for a quick and easy comparison of the relative number of deaths for each cause.

What are its use cases?
The visualization is useful for anyone who wants to gain a high-level understanding of the deadliest causes of death in the world over the last 30 years. It could be used by healthcare professionals, researchers, policymakers, and the general public to identify and understand the most significant causes of mortality globally. It could also be used in educational settings to teach students about global health and disease burden.

Death Rates vs GDP & Health Expenditure (per capita)

By Country, 2000-2019

Why this visualisation?
This visualization is created to explore the relationship between a country's healthcare expenditure, GDP per capita, and death rates. By plotting the data points on a scatter plot, we can see if there is any correlation between these variables.

What is it visualising?
The visualization is showing a scatter plot with GDP per capita on the x-axis, healthcare expenditure per capita on the y-axis, and death rates represented by the size of the dots. By looking at the plot, we can see the relationship between these three variables for different countries over time.

How is it visualising?
The scatter plot timeline is an interactive visualization that displays data points for each country over a 20-year period. The size of the dots represents the death rates of the country. The coordinates are used to color the points, creating clusters among the continents and making the countries easier to locate. Users can hover over the points to view the country's information.

What are some of its use cases?
It can be useful for researchers studying the impact of healthcare spending on death rates across countries over time. Additionally, it can be used by the general public to gain insights into how their country compares to others in terms of healthcare expenditure and death rates.

Death Rate from Smoking

Estimated annual number of deaths attributed to smoking per 100,000 people.

Why this visualization?
This visualization is useful for understanding the global trends in smoking-related deaths over a 30-year period. It allows researchers and policymakers to identify the countries and continents that are most affected by smoking-related deaths and to track changes and trends in smoking patterns over time. This information can be used to develop targeted interventions to reduce smoking-related deaths.

What is it visualizing?
The visualization is visualizing the number of smoking-related deaths per 100,000 people on a world map. It captures and displays the overall picture of smoking-related deaths and enables users to identify the countries and continents with the highest and lowest rates of smoking-related deaths. Additionally, the visualization is interactive and dynamic, allowing users to change the year and see the trend of smoking-related deaths over 30 years.

How is it visualizing?
The visualization is using a world map to display the number of smoking-related deaths per 100,000 people. The color-coding of the countries also helps to quickly differentiate between the countries that have a higher number of smoking-related deaths and those that have a lower number. The slider at the bottom of the visualization makes it easy to navigate through the different years and understand the changes that have occurred over time. Hovering over a specific country highlights its borders and displays a tooltip that contains relevant information about the country, such as its name and the number of smoking-related deaths that occurred in the year that the slider is on. The interactivity of the visualization makes it easier for users to explore and gain insights into the data.
